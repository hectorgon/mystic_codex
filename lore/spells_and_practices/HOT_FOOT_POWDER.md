# Hot Foot Powder

**Purpose:** A potent banishing formula used to drive away enemies, unwanted neighbors, or abusive individuals, forcing them to leave town or move out of their home.

## Components
- Hot Foot Powder (often composed of crushed red pepper, salt, sulfur, and sometimes powdered John the Conqueror or Devil's Shoestring).
- Alternatively: "War water powder" or pigeon eggs (for flight).
- The target's footprint or the threshold of their door.

## The Ritual

### Method 1: The Doorstep Banishing
1. Wait until midnight or 1:00 AM when the target is asleep.
2. Take the Hot Foot Powder (which may look like white sugar depending on the mixture) and approach the target's house in absolute silence.
3. Sprinkle the powder in a straight line across their doorway or exactly where they must step when leaving the house the next morning.
4. Command the powder: *"I lay you here to work, do what I tell you to do. Run them away."*
5. Turn around and walk away immediately. Do not speak another word until you return home. When the target steps over the powder, the condition will attach to them and force them to move.

### Method 2: The Shoe Spell (Crippling or Banishing)
1. If you have access to the target's shoes, lift the inner sole (the leather rag inside).
2. Sprinkle the Hot Foot Powder, War Water Powder, or powdered Devil's Shoestring underneath the insole.
3. Replace the insole so the powder is hidden. When the target wears the shoes and their feet begin to sweat, the spell activates. It will either cause their feet to swell and ache terribly, or it will instil an overwhelming urge to wander, effectively running them out of town.

## Lore & Context
> *"Hot Foot Powder is the ultimate eviction notice. It works on the principle of sympathetic magic: you introduce a painful, restless, fiery element to the soles of their feet, and their physical body has no choice but to mimic the spiritual irritation by moving, wandering, and running."* — **CERNUNNOS**

Hot Foot Powder (and its liquid cousin, Hot Foot Oil) is one of the most famous malevolent/banishing tools in the tradition. While it is primarily used to remove enemies, variations exist to force an unfaithful partner to leave their lover, or to clear a house of bad tenants. The powder is often sprinkled where it cannot be avoided, relying on the target to "pick it up" through their feet.
