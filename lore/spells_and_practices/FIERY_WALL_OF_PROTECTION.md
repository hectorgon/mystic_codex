# Fiery Wall of Protection

**Purpose:** A protective practice to banish evil spirits, dispel crossed conditions, and keep enemies or the law away from a home by utilizing the purifying and aggressive energy of fire and sulfur.

## Components
- Sulfur (brimstone)
- Black candle(s)
- Incense (often mixed with bluestone, gunpowder, or aloes)
- Optional: Cow manure or dog hair (for aggressive banishing)

## The Ritual

### Method 1: The Black Candle
1. Take a black candle and place it in a dark or hidden corner of your house, or in a back shed where the open air can hit it.
2. Light the candle, allowing its smoke and energy to penetrate the dark corners where evil might hide. 
3. The light and smoke drive away evil and protect the home from malicious workings.

### Method 2: The Sulfur Ring & Fumigation
1. **Fumigation:** Every morning, sprinkle a small amount of sulfur on a hot stove. As the sulfur burns, allow the scent to permeate the house. This is said to drive the devil and evil spirits away.
2. **The Ring:** To protect a specific item or area (like buried treasure or an object you fear will be stolen), make a circular line of sulfur around it. No spirit or enemy can cross this ring.
3. **The Doorstep:** Alternatively, mix sulfur with a little lard and spread it across your doorstep every morning to prevent harm from entering.

### Method 3: Aggressive Banishing (The "Fiery" Wall)
1. For severe cases involving the law or persistent enemies, gather incense, bluestone, and gunpowder.
2. Burn this mixture together in the home. The smoke creates a literal and spiritual barrier that keeps unwelcome authorities and enemies at bay.

## Lore & Context
> *"The element of fire in Southern conjuration is not gentle; it is a weapon. When you burn sulfur or gunpowder, you are not just clearing the air—you are declaring war on whatever seeks to harm you. The scent of brimstone is the scent of boundaries being violently enforced."* — **ANTIGRAVITY**

The "Fiery Wall of Protection" as a concept often revolves around the aggressive use of fire, sulfur (brimstone), and pungent smoke. While modern practitioners might use a pre-made oil, the traditional roots found in the corpus emphasize the raw act of burning these potent materials to fumigate the home and cast out negativity.

## Source Lineage

> *(Hoodoo Conjuration Witchcraft & Rootwork Vol 5, Page 695)*

> "Take sulphur and a little lard and spread 'cross your door - doorstep every .arning [morning], and it'll harm you... if somebody did that to my door or - something to harm me..."
> *(Hoodoo Conjuration Witchcraft & Rootwork Vol 5, Page 699)*

> "Well, they claim that if you take gunpowder and bluestone and aloes... And burn incense along with that. And burn all of it together, it'll have an effect, a tendency to keep bootleggers [he means the police] away from your house..."
> *(Hoodoo Conjuration Witchcraft & Rootwork Vol 5, Page 304)*
