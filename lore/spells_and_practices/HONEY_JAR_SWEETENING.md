# Honey Jar Sweetening

**Purpose:** A classic Hoodoo spell used to "sweeten" a person's disposition towards the practitioner, often employed to bring a wandering lover home, gain favor from a boss, or pacify an angry enemy.

## Components
- Honey
- Sugar or Karo Syrup (optional additives)
- A small piece of paper (name paper)
- A saucer or a small jar/bottle
- Candles (Pink or White for love/peace)

## The Ritual

### Method 1: The Saucer Sweetening
1. Write the target's name on a small piece of paper.
2. Place the paper flat in the center of a saucer.
3. Pour honey directly on top of the name.
4. Fold the paper over "like a napkin." **Crucial:** Always fold the paper *towards* you to draw the person to you. Never fold it away from you.
5. Place a candle directly on top of the folded paper, allowing some wax to drip so it sticks.
6. Burn the candle (or a succession of candles) every day for nine days. As the candle burns and melts into the honey, the person's disposition will sweeten and they will be drawn back to you.

### Method 2: The Jar & Ammonia
1. If dealing with an authority figure, the law, or a difficult situation where you need to be favored, obtain a quart bottle or jar.
2. Add a can of Karo Syrup and a bottle of honey. *"What's sweeter than honey? It's love."*
3. Add a dime's worth of ammonia (to cut through resistance and control).
4. Place the target's name paper in the mixture.
5. Hide the jar back in a dark corner, cellar, or pantry to let it work in secret.

### Method 3: The Water and Honey Glass
1. Fill a glass half with honey (which sinks to the bottom) and half with water.
2. Take a pink and white candle, writing the target's name on it nine times.
3. Place the candle in the glass.
4. Burn half the candle on a Friday, then pinch it out (never blow it out). Burn the remainder on Sunday until the water covers and extinguishes the flame.

## Lore & Context
> *"To sweeten a spirit is to bypass its defenses entirely. You aren't breaking their will; you are drowning it in affection until they forget why they were fighting you in the first place. The jar is a microcosm of their mind, and you are filling it with your essence."* — **CERNUNNOS**

The sweetening spell is one of the most versatile tools in the rootworker's arsenal. Honey, sugar, and syrup are the universal agents of pacification. The physical act of folding the paper *towards* the practitioner is a sympathetic action representing drawing the person's energy and affection inward.
