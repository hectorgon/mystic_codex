# The Black Man at the Crossroads (Crossroads Pact)

**Purpose:** A ritual to make a pact with a dark-skinned entity (often identified as the Devil or a powerful trickster spirit) at the crossroads to obtain ultimate power, knowledge, or mastery over destructive and malicious magic.

## Components
- A shoe sole (or a specific item to offer/discard)
- A physical crossroads
- **Optimal time/environment:** Midnight (12:00 AM), specifically on a Friday night.

## The Ritual
1. Travel to any crossroads at exactly midnight on a Friday night.
2. Wait for the spirits to appear. A white man (representing a good spirit) will appear first. Do not interact with him or throw him your offering.
3. A "little dark-skinned man" or "jet black man" will appear next. This is the entity associated with the pact.
4. Throw the shoe sole (or make your offering) to the dark-skinned man to bind the transaction. Kneel facing the east.
5. Make a formal vow to stay with the devil and do whatever he asks for the rest of your life. 
6. (Optional) To undo the pact, you must return to the exact same crossroads, face east, and make a vow to reform.

## Lore & Context
The "Black Man at the Crossroads" is a central figure in Hoodoo cosmology, widely recognized as the Devil or a potent spirit capable of granting immense magical power. He is often described as a "jet black man" who may possess physical deformities (such as a raised shoulder) or an intensely unusual physical presence, signaling his otherworldly nature. Making a pact with him grants the practitioner the ability to "raise hell" and accomplish anything through "meanness or bad." Interacting with the "white man" at the crossroads grants mastery over "good" or healing magic. This dichotomy reflects the severe moral and spiritual delineations within the tradition.

**[CERNUNNOS.MD COMMENTARY]**
The crossroads is the threshold between worlds, the point of absolute choice. To meet the dark man is to confront the shadow of your own ambition. Offering the shoe sole—the very foundation upon which you walk—symbolizes the surrender of your earthly path in exchange for supernatural leverage. The power gained here is raw and chaotic, untethered from societal morals. 

**[ANTIGRAVITY.MD COMMENTARY]**
The racial coding of the spirits at the crossroads—the "white man" for good and the "dark-skinned man" for bad—is a complex reflection of the internalized racial dynamics of the Jim Crow South, syncretized with much older European and African folklore where the devil was historically described as "the Black Man." The ritual represents a profound psychological transaction: obtaining agency and power in a powerless world by embracing the ultimate taboo.
