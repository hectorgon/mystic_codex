# Prototype: The Crossroads Ritual

**Purpose:** A foundational Hoodoo practice used to discard the remnants of spellwork, seek the intercession of crossroads spirits (often associated with Papa Legba or the Black Man of the Crossroads), and open paths or acquire new skills.

## Components
- The ritual remnants or offerings (e.g., graveyard dirt, nine pennies, black coffee, or whiskey).
- A physical intersection of two dirt or paved roads (a 4-way intersection is traditional, though Y-intersections or 3-way forks are sometimes used depending on the specific working).
- **Time/Environment:** Midnight or right before dawn. Solitude is strictly required.

## The Ritual
1. **Approach the Intersection:** Walk to the center of the crossroads at the designated time. Do not speak to anyone on your way there.
2. **The Offering:** Toss the offering or spell remnants directly into the center of the cross. If paying a spirit for a request, toss the coins over your left shoulder.
3. **The Request:** State your desire clearly. If you are there to learn a skill (such as playing the guitar), begin playing your instrument until the entity arrives to tune it or guide your hands.
4. **The Departure:** Turn around and walk home without looking back. Looking back is said to break the spell or invite the spirit to follow you home.

## Lore & Context
> *“The crossroads is not just a place on a map; it is a fracture in the geometry of the physical plane where the energy bodies of spirits and humans can safely intersect. To stand at the crossroads is to stand outside of linear time.”* — **ANTIGRAVITY**

The Crossroads ritual represents the intersection of the physical and spiritual realms. It is arguably the most recognizable practice in Hoodoo and Southern conjuration, functioning both as a disposal method for dangerous items (where the converging energies disperse the spell) and a meeting place for pacts and power. Variations exist where the practitioner must return to the same crossroads for nine consecutive nights to achieve their request.
