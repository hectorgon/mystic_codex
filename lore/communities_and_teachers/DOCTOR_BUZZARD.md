# Doctor Buzzard

**Role:** A legendary and incredibly powerful root doctor from the South Carolina/Georgia region, famous for his mastery over the court system and his ability to effortlessly evade law enforcement and imprisonment.

## Background
- **Location/Region:** Beaufort, South Carolina / Georgia Coast
- **Known associations:** Often associated with animal familiars, specifically buzzards and snakes.
- **Specialty or reputation:** Known for getting people out of jail, winning impossible court cases, shape-shifting, and projecting his spiritual presence into courtrooms.

## Legal and Magical Encounters
Doctor Buzzard is described as a force that completely subverted the legal system. He was rarely recorded as appearing physically in court. Instead, he would send substitutes—snakes, birds, or other animals—that would physically disrupt the proceedings. In one notable account, as a judge was about to pass sentence on one of his clients, two snakes spiraled into the courtroom, clearing the room and preventing the sentence from being completed, effectively freeing the prisoner. In another account, he was locked in a jail cell, but by morning he had vanished, leaving only a buzzard in his place.

## Lore & Context
**[CERNUNNOS.MD COMMENTARY]**
Doctor Buzzard is the archetype of the sovereign magician. He does not fight the law on its own terms; he simply overrides its reality. By replacing himself with a buzzard in a locked cell, or sending snakes to halt a judge's sentence, he demonstrates that his magical jurisdiction supersedes the state's jurisdiction. He is a master of the environment.

**[ANTIGRAVITY.MD COMMENTARY]**
The legend of Doctor Buzzard serves as a powerful psychological talisman for the community. In a legal system where black defendants were almost guaranteed conviction in the Jim Crow South, the mythos of a rootworker who could paralyze judges, escape locked cells, and command animals offered a profound sense of protective power and subversive justice. He represented the ultimate triumph over an oppressive system.

Everyone in the courtroom, including the judge, rushed outside the courthouse. This inability of the judge to begin or complete the sentence automatically freed the prisoner."

> *(Hoodoo Conjuration Witchcraft & Rootwork Vol 1, Page 353)*
