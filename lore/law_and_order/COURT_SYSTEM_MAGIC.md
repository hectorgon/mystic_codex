# Court System Magic

**Summary:** The courtroom was a central battleground in rootwork, where practitioners employed extensive magical rituals to influence judges, confuse juries, and ensure the acquittal of defendants, often circumventing the mundane legal process entirely.

## Elements of the Account
- **Key figures involved:** Judges, district attorneys, juries, root doctors, and defendants.
- **Methods used (legal or magical):** Bribery, silencing spells (using beef tongues pinned with needles), "tying down" the court, making enemies appear drunk, turning furniture upside down, wearing inside-out clothing, and using powerful physical manifestations (like summoning snakes or birds).
- **Outcomes:** Cases dismissed, juries hung, sentences interrupted, and outright acquittals achieved through spiritual dominance over the physical court.

## The Narrative / Practice
1. **Controlling the Judge and Jury:** Practitioners frequently targeted the names of the judge and hostile witnesses. Common practices involved writing these names nine times and placing them in a shoe, or writing them on a red candle and burning it. To silence a jury or prosecutor, their names might be placed inside a beef tongue secured with nine needles and nine pins, symbolizing the binding of their tongues.
2. **Environmental Disruption:** On the morning of a trial, a defendant might turn their bed upside down or pile all their chairs onto it to "upset" the case. Inside the courtroom, bottles containing chloroform, ephedrine, and brimstone might be secretly opened to sway the room's atmosphere in their favor.
3. **Mundane Interventions (Bribery):** The texts also openly acknowledge the intersection of magic and mundane corruption. Informants noted that if a root doctor had enough money, the "work" might simply consist of privately bribing the judge to dismiss the case.

## Lore & Context
**[CERNUNNOS.MD COMMENTARY]**
The court is nothing more than a theater of power. The judge sits on high, wrapped in a black robe, wielding a gavel like a wand. Rootworkers recognized this for what it was: a competing ritual. By bringing their own ritual implements into the space—bound tongues, graveyard dirt, and the names of their enemies beneath their feet—they spiritually inverted the hierarchy of the room.

**[ANTIGRAVITY.MD COMMENTARY]**
These extensive "court case" spells demonstrate a systemic mistrust of the legal system by marginalized communities. When justice is inaccessible or historically weaponized against you, magical intervention becomes the only logical defense. It is notable that the magic targets the *mechanisms* of the court—the judge's voice, the jury's cohesion, the physical paperwork—rather than abstract notions of "truth."
