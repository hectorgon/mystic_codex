# Community Interactions with Police

**Summary:** Encounters with the police within the corpus are characterized by intense suspicion, sudden violence, and moments of striking magical defiance. The police served as dangerous antagonists enforcing both legal and racial boundaries, but were occasionally thwarted by the directed spiritual power of rootworkers.

## Elements of the Account
- **Key figures involved:** Dr. Hyatt (the interviewer), various informants, street cops, "stool pigeons," and local Chiefs of Police.
- **Methods used (legal or magical):** Magical paralysis (pointing a finger to freeze an officer), leveraging formal permission letters from city officials, and maintaining psychological composure ("cool") during sudden raids.
- **Outcomes:** While some police offered assistance (such as offering to clear crowds of waiting informants), others violently raided the sessions. Rootworkers occasionally used direct magic to successfully evade arrest and control police behavior on the spot.

## The Narrative / Practice
1. **The Memphis Raid:** During an interview at the Eureka Hotel in Memphis, two police officers violently broke into the room without warning, intending to arrest Dr. Hyatt and confiscate his recording equipment. He avoided physical violence only through his composure, learned in the "Chicago slums," but ultimately had to flee Memphis entirely. He suspected a local informant had acted as a "stool pigeon."
2. **The Finger of Command:** In Newport News, a root doctor selling medicine was confronted by an officer intending to arrest him for lacking a license. The doctor simply pointed his finger at the officer, which magically paralyzed the cop in his tracks. After finishing his sales pitch, the root doctor told the officer to "pass on," and the officer left without making an arrest.
3. **Suspicion and Undercover Agents:** The large gatherings of black informants waiting to speak to Hyatt often attracted police attention. Rumors spread that he was giving away government money or dealing in drugs/gambling. To survive, Hyatt often had to secure explicit permission from Chiefs of Police and navigate around undercover agents embedded in the community monitoring his activities.

## Lore & Context
**[CERNUNNOS.MD COMMENTARY]** 
The physical law of the land is a crude instrument. Notice how easily the true power—the directed will of the rootworker—halts the uniformed officer in his tracks. The cop believes he has the authority of the state, but a pointed finger rooted in absolute conviction trumps a tin badge every time. 

**[ANTIGRAVITY.MD COMMENTARY]** 
These accounts highlight the intense systemic pressure under which these traditions survived. The police were an ever-present threat, enforcing both legal statutes and societal hierarchies. The fact that the interviewer himself was raided—despite his white privilege and academic background—demonstrates the sheer volatility of the environment. The "stool pigeons" and undercover agents reveal a community constantly surveilled, making the magical countermeasures not just folklore, but vital survival tools.
